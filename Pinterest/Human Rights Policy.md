Pinterest Human Rights Statement



At Pinterest we are committed to creating a positive place online where everyone feels welcomed and can find

inspiration to create a life they love. We strive to deliver an experience that reflects our users interests, tastes

and cultures—no matter who they are. We cannot deliver that experience if we do not inspire belonging and

positivity through our product. To achieve this, we are deliberate about creating and investing in

industry-leading product features that foster inclusivity and experiences that are correlated with positive

emotional wellbeing outcomes. Upholding human rights and creating belonging for all is foundational to

building a sustainable and thriving business.



Our commitment



In furtherance of our mission and core values, Pinterest is committed to operating ethically and responsibly,

treating everyone with dignity, and respecting the rights of all individuals. We strive to conduct business in

alignment with the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the United Nations’ Guiding Principles on

Business and Human Rights.



Below we’ve outlined our policies that aim to promote and support the fundamental human rights across our

value chain. This statement consolidates our existing commitments and practices relating to the protection of

human rights.



Our relevant policies



Our commitment to human rights, encompassing principles of equality, safety, dignity, privacy, and voice, is

exemplified through:



● Our Community Guidelines, which outline what we do and don’t allow on Pinterest. We seek

guidance from subject matter experts to inform and update our guidelines.

● Our Developer Guidelines, which outline what we do and don’t allow when building

applications and services with Pinterest Materials (like developer features, tools and APIs). We

require developers to follow these guidelines as well as our other terms and policies, such as

our Developer and API Terms of Service and our Community Guidelines.

● Our Supplier Code of Conduct, which sets out our expectations for our suppliers’ business

conduct when working with or for Pinterest. The Supplier Code of Conduct unequivocally

states Pinterest’s commitment to respecting human rights, including the principles recognized

in international human rights standards like the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and

preventing modern forms of slavery and human trafficking in our supply chains or business.

● Our Modern Slavery Act Transparency Statement, which describes our policies and

practices with respect to combating slavery and human trafficking on our platform and in our

business operations and supply chains.

● Our Code of Business Conduct \& Ethics, which sets Pinterest’s expectations for how we

treat each other, our Pinners, advertisers, content providers and suppliers. It also informs the

decisions we make about how to run our business with Pinterest’s mission and values as our

guiding light.

● Our Transparency Reports, published biannually, which includes information on our efforts to

keep our platform safe and inspiring, such as the number of Pin and account deactivations. It

also includes insight into the volume of information and deactivation requests we received from

law enforcement and government entities.

● Our Inclusion and Diversity practices. We are deeply committed to fostering strong diversity

and inclusion practices within our workplace, recognizing their pivotal role as drivers of

equality. Embracing diverse perspectives is not only essential for a harmonious workplace but

also serves as a crucial input in developing products and policies that bring equal value to all

individuals.

● Our Advertising Guidelines and Merchant Guidelines, which explain additional policies that

apply to ads and merchants on Pinterest.

● Our Privacy Policy and Law Enforcement Guidelines, which explain how we use personal

data and respond to requests for user information from law enforcement agencies, courts, etc.

We conduct regular reviews of these policies, paying close attention to our data usage

practices and the implementation of appropriate safeguards to address potential concerns

effectively. Furthermore, as part of our policies, we diligently evaluate whether these requests

align with internationally recognized standards on human rights, encompassing principles of

due process, privacy, free expression, and the rule of law.

● Our call to action with the Inspired Internet Pledge in collaboration with the Digital Wellness

Lab at Boston Children's Hospital, to unite with the common goal of making the internet a safer

and healthier place for everyone, especially young people. The Inspired Internet Pledge

provides a framework for companies to take meaningful, measurable actions to support

positive mental and emotional wellbeing outcomes both on and offline.



Pinterest has other formal corporate policies reflecting our commitment to prevent violations of human rights

in our business, including our Respect in the Workplace Policy, Global Purchasing Policy, and Sanctions \&

Export Control Policy.



Grievance mechanisms



Pinterest is committed to transparency in our approach to respecting human rights. Pinterest’s Supplier Code

of Conduct and Code of Business Conduct \& Ethics offer multiple reporting channels for employees and our

external workforce. We will not tolerate retaliation against anyone for seeking advice, making a good-faith

report of suspected misconduct or for participating in an investigation of suspected misconduct.



Pinterest also maintains an anonymous reporting hotline and web portal for employees, contractors, clients,

suppliers, and the public to report conduct that might violate Pinterest’s policies or the law, including

concerns about human rights, slavery or human trafficking in Pinterest’s supply chains or business operations.

All of Pinterest’s reporting channels are listed in the Code of Business Conduct \& Ethics and our Supplier

Code of Conduct.

by multiple teams across Pinterest.



The implementation of the actions, commitments and policies referenced in this statement is overseen by the

oversight of environmental, social and governance (ESG) matters.
